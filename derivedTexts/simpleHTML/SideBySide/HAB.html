Note: This is still a very early look into the unfinished text of the Open English Translation of the Bible. Please double-check the text before using in public. Some things (like capitalisation of “him” referring to Jesus or “father” referring to God) in the RV haven’t been decided yet so we’re still testing both ways.

Note: The Readers’ Version on the left is a translation into contemporary English aimed at the person on the street who hasn’t necessarily been brought up with exposure to Biblical jargon and/or King James English. It’s designed to be used alongside the Literal Version on the right which gives the English reader a window into what’s actually written in the original languages. (See the introduction for more details—we recommend that you read the introduction first if you’re wanting to fully understand the Literal Version.) By comparing the left and right columns, you should be able to easily get the message of the text, while at the same time keeping an eye on what it was actually translated from.

Habakkuk

It is our prayer that the Open English Translation of the Bible will give you clear understanding of the messages written by the inspired Biblical writers.

Introduction

Te seini baseen, egkabasa ta ka pegpalalahey of Prupita Habakuk te Magbebaye God. Diye mig-ugpe si Habakuk te Huda and nasulat buwa seini baseen te me subla te hen‑em hatus leg‑un (600) te ware pad neetew si Kristu. Ne mig-inse si Habakuk te God ke mania te ware din legpari seeye se me karumaan din me matig-Israel migpandeeg-deeg te me meupiya people. Migtabak ka God egpeuyan sikandin te me sundalu me matig-Babilunya eyew te peglegpad kandan. Te sika timpu, amana ikaaldek ka me sundalu te nasud Babilunya tenged su amana sikandan mabbulut and natalu ran ka nasud te Asirya and Egypt. Ware nakasabut si Habakuk ke mania te iling due ka inggimu te Magbebaye God. Ne mig-inse sikandin te God miggenendue, “Mania te imparuyun-duyunan nu ka people kene matinumanen? Ne mania te egpeeneng-eneng ka ke egdereetan dan ka people matareng pad kandan?” (1:13). Ne migtabak ka God egbulig sikandin te eleg timpu, sasangan pad: “…ka me matareng, egkeuyag mule tenged te pegpalintutuu ran.” (2:4)

The duma baad te seini baseen iyan ka pegtagne te egginguma karereetan te seeye se maddeet person. Ne minsan egkaaldek si Habakuk egdusaan e te God ka Huda, piru migpalintutuu red sikandin egluwasen din ded ka keet-etawan din and egbehayan te kanekal egpakaantus te keyirapan egkeula-ula ran. Mig-impusan ka seini baseen te ulahingen te pegdayan te Magbebaye maresen.
