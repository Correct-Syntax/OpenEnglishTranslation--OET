Note: This is still a very early look into the unfinished text of the Open English Translation of the Bible. Please double-check the text in advance before using in public. Some things (like capitalisation of “him” referring to Jesus or “father” referring to God) in the RV haven’t been decided yet so we’re still testing both ways.

Note: The Readers’ Version on the left is a translation into contemporary English aimed at the person on the street who hasn’t necessarily been brought up with exposure to Biblical jargon and/or 16th century English. It’s designed to be used alongside the Literal Version on the right which gives the English reader a window into what’s actually written in the original languages. (See the introduction for more details—we recommend that you read the introduction first if you’re wanting to fully understand the Literal Version.) By comparing the left and right columns, you should be able to easily get the message of the text, while at the same time keeping an eye on what it was actually translated from.

Introduction

Si Isayas anak of Amus ka migsulat te this baseen. Sabeka sikandin prupita te God, and diye sikandin mig-ugpe te Jerusalem. The kaluwasan te ngaran ‘Isayas’, ‘The Master God ka Egluwas.’ Nasulat ka this baseen te me leg‑un 740 peendiye te 680 te ware pad neetew si Jesus. Ne nabaad this te tatelu baad:

1.) Kapitulu 1–39: Nahuhud here ka concerning the timpu ingginalatan te Asirya ka Huda, ka migharian diye te igkakawanan balabahan. Ne nakita of Isayas tenged bes te sale te keet-etawan, te pegkamasinupaken dan, and te kulang ka pegsalig dan te God, ka egpuunan ke mania te migginalatan te Asirya ka Huda. Impaay-ayaran panulua of Isayas ka keet-etawan and ka me pangulu ran eg-ugpe sikandan matareng se hinimuwan and eleg se peggukum. Impanpanayan din sikandan emun ke kene sikandan egpammineg te lalag te God, egkengareetan iya sikandan. Piru intagne again of Isayas ka birth te descendants of Dabid te egginguma timpu iyan likes eg-ikulen te keet-etawan, and egginguma ka timpu due kalinaw te intiru world.

2.) Kapitulu 40–55: Natagne here ka concerning the egginguma pegkarakep and pegkeuyan te masulug keet-etawan te Huda peendiye te Babilunya. Ne impanengneng of prupita Isayas minsan iling dutu, kene egmahuye ka keet-etawan kene egkaawaan te egkapallateng su egluwasen ded te God ka keet-etawan din and igpalibed din ded sikandan diye te Jerusalem eyew egbunsud e man e te iyam peg-ugpe. The uluwan te this me kapitulu, ka God ka egbuut te egkeyitabu and ka egplanu for te keet-etawan din, and miglagkes also this te katuyuan dan diye te langun nations egpakarawat te panalangin puun te God pinaahi te Israel.

3.) Kapitulu 56–66: Natagne also here ka concerning the keugpe te people igpalibed ded te God diye te Jerusalem. Nahuhud of Isayas here ka me suhu te God concerning the matareng peg-ugpe, eleg peggukum, pegtuman te Aldew te Peggimeley, pegpanubad, and praying. Ne egkabasa te this me kapitulu ka lalag inggamit of Jesus te pegpanengneng in-alam sikandin in order to pegwali te meupiya story (Isayas 61:1-2; Lukas 4:18-19.)
