Note: This is still a very early look into the unfinished text of the Open English Translation of the Bible. Please double-check the text before using in public. Some things (like capitalisation of “him” referring to Jesus or “father” referring to God) in the RV haven’t been decided yet so we’re still testing both ways.

Note: The Readers’ Version on the left is a translation into contemporary English aimed at the person on the street who hasn’t necessarily been brought up with exposure to Biblical jargon and/or King James English. It’s designed to be used alongside the Literal Version on the right which gives the English reader a window into what’s actually written in the original languages. (See the introduction for more details—we recommend that you read the introduction first if you’re wanting to fully understand the Literal Version.) By comparing the left and right columns, you should be able to easily get the message of the text, while at the same time keeping an eye on what it was actually translated from.

Nahum

It is our prayer that the Open English Translation of the Bible will give you clear understanding of the messages written by the inspired Biblical writers.

Introduction

The seini baseen, insulatan te story concerning the karereetan te Ninibi, ka iney siyudad te Asirya kuntere dengan te Israel. Nasulat seini te me elat-elat te 663 and 612 leg‑un te ware pad neetew ka Kristu. Nahale si prupita Nahum te nareetan e ka Ninibi, su sika ka palinneu eglegparan iya te Magbebaye God ka me makasesale. Minsan ka people, migpalintutuu degma inlegparan te God ka Ninibi su mabbulut ma and me hambuhen ka me mahinged te sika siyudad.
