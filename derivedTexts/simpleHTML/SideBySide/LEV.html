Note: The Readers’ Version on the left is a translation into contemporary English aimed at the person on the street who hasn’t necessarily been brought up with exposure to Biblical jargon and/or King James English. (See the introduction for more details—we recommend that you read the introduction first if you’re wanting to fully understand the Literal Version.) By comparing the left and right columns, you should be able to easily get the message of the text, while at the same time keeping an eye on what it was actually translated from.

Leviticus

It is our prayer that the Open English Translation of the Bible will give you clear understanding of the messages written by the inspired Biblical writers.

Introduction

The kaluwasan te Libitiku iyan ka “The in-umew rin” iyan ka me talagpanubad eyew te pegpatuman te me balaud imbehey te God kandan.

Impalintutuuwan te people si Muwisis ded ka migsulat te seini libru diye te Bubungan Sinay te timpu te nangekampu sikandan diye, su egkabasa seini diye te: 1:1, 4:1, 6:1,24, and te 8:1.

The Libitiku baseen concerning seini te eleg egbatasanen te descendants of Israel eyew eg-isipen sikandan malinis te God. The sabeka paahi eg-isipen sikandan malinis te God iyan ka pegpanubad te igkeupii te God.

Ne eyew egkaay-ayaran iya ka pegpanubad, mig-alam ka God te person si Aarun and te descendants din.

Main components of this “book”

The me balaud concerning the me halad and te me pegpanubad 1:1-7:38

The peghalad engki Aarun and te me anak din me lukes eyew egkeyimu me talagpanubad 8:1-10:20

The me balaud concerning the peglinis and te kene malinis 11:1-15:33

The aldew te pegpasaylu te sale 16:1-34

The me balaud concerning the pegpamatulus te pegkeetew and te pegsimba 17:1-27:34
