Note: This is still a very early look into the unfinished text of the Open English Translation of the Bible. Please double-check the text before using in public.

Note: This Readers’ Version is a translation into contemporary English aimed at the person on the street who hasn’t necessarily been brought up with exposure to Biblical jargon and/or King James English. It’s designed to be used alongside the Literal Version which gives the English reader a window into what’s actually written in the original languages. (See the introduction for more details—we recommend that you read the introduction first if you’re wanting to read and understand the Literal Version.) Between the two versions you should be able to easily read the clear message of the text, while at the same time being able to check for yourself if that’s a fair translation of what the original authors had inked onto their ancient manuscripts.

1 Samuel

Introduction

Nahuhud te seini baseen ka concerning the keet-etawan te nasud Israel te timpu si Samwil pad ka pangulu ran taman te nakasubal e si Saul migpangulu kandan. Ne si Saul ka first Hari. Nahuhud degma te seini baseen ke nekey ka egpuunan naawaan si Saul te katungdanan din isip Hari, and impasubalan e sikandin te God ki Dabid.

Te ware pad Hari dengan te Israel, iyan pad in-isip te people Hari dan ka God. Piru mighangyu sikandan te God egbehayan te Hari egpangulu kandan. Ne igkeupii te God egparuma-ruma ka Hari and ka people te pegmandu din and te kandin peggukum. Su ka langun katenged te person, meyaman ma and se egkaayu-ayu, keilangan egpekeuyun te Balaud te God.

The uluwan te seini baseen sika se pegkamatinumanen te God diye egpangkus te kapanaluwan, and ka pegsupak te igkeupii te God diye egpangkus te karereetan. Naay-ayaran sika nalalag te God diye te kapitulu 2:30 miggenendue, “The migtahud keddi, egtahuren ku degma and ka miglemet keddi, eglemetan ku degma.”
