John

Introduction

Author

This account about the works and teachings of Jesus was written by John, brother of James--both of them were in the small group of twelve close followers of Jesus that he selected as His apprentices and who accompanied Him around as He taught. Out of them, John was the one that greatly loved Jesus, and his closeness gave him much of the insight that he includes with his description of Jesus’ actions and teaching. The two brothers were the sons of Zebedee, and they had all worked on the Lake of Galilee as fishermen.

This account

We place John’s account before the others because it begins with the eternal existence of Jesus the Messiah. Not only is Jesus the godly messenger, he was also the creator who has now descended from heaven as a man. Omitting any mention of the baby Jesus, John does however tell us about John the Immerser who came before Jesus to prepare the people for the arrival of the promised Messiah.

1:1 The coming of the true light

1In the beginning was the Messenger, and the Messenger was with God, and the Messenger was God. 2 The Messenger was with God even at the creation. 3 Everything came into existence through the Messenger—not even one thing came into existence apart from Him. 4 He holds Life, and that Life is the Light of humankind. 5 And that Light is shining in the darkness, and darkness cannot overcome Him.

6 [ref]A man arrived who had been sent by God. His name was John. 7 He came as a testimony to tell about the Light so that everyone would believe the Light. 8 John himself wasn’t the Light—he was merely the announcer of the Light. 9 The true Light which enlightens every person was coming into the world.

10 He was in the world and He was the creator of the world, and yet the world didn’t even know Him. 11 He came to His own chosen people, but they wouldn’t accept Him. 12 But whoever would accept Him, whoever trusted in His authority, He gave them the right to become children of God. 13 Not physical children and not coming from human actions, but children of God.

14 And the Messenger became a human and lived here among us and we saw his greatness—the greatness of an only child of the Father—full of grace and truth.

15 John explained about him and loudly told everyone, “This is the one I meant when I said, ‘There’s someone coming after me, yet who came before me, because he existed before I did!’ ”

16 Because He has so much, we all received grace and then even more grace. 17 The commandments came to us through Moses, but grace and truth came to us through Jesus the Messiah. 18 No one has ever actually seen God, but the human son born from God the father, He was the one to explain God to us.

5 But his mother told the servants, “Do whatever he tells you.”

6 Now there were six empty stone water jars there that are used for Jewish purification ceremonies. (Each one could hold over 70 litres.) 7 So Jesus told them to fill the jars with water, and they filled them up. 8 Then he said, “Ok, now take a sample out and give it to the MC.” So they took some. 9 And when the MC tasted the water that had now turned to wine (but he didn’t know where it had come from), he called the groom 10 and exclaimed, “Most people supply the best wine at the beginning, and the cheaper stuff when people are a bit tipsy. But you’ve kept the best wine until last!”

3:1 Jesus tells Nicodemus about the need to be reborn

3 Jesus responded, “I’ll tell you frankly that no one can enter God’s kingdom unless he is reborn.”

4 Nicodemus questioned, “How could an old person be reborn? He can’t just return to his mother’s womb and then get born again!”

5 And Jesus continued, “Honestly, I’m telling you: unless a person is born from both water and the spirit, they can’t enter God’s kingdom. 6 Someone that’s born from a body is flesh, but someone that’s born from God’s spirit is spiritual. 7 You shouldn’t be surprised that I told you that everyone needs to be reborn. 8 The wind blows wherever it wants, and you hear its sound but you can’t actually see where it’s coming from and where it’s going. 9 Nicodemus replied, “How could all this happen?”

10 And Jesus answered, “You’re a Jewish teacher and yet you don’t know all this? 11 It’s true that we just teach what we know, and explain what we’ve seen ourselves, but you people aren’t accepting our testimony. 12 If I tell you all earthly things and you don’t believe them, how will you ever believe if I explain heavenly things? 13 None of you have gone up into heaven, except that I, the Son of Man, have come down from heaven.

14 [ref]“Just like Moses lifted up that bronze snake on a pole in the wilderness, it’s necessary for the Son of Man to be lifted up, 15 so that everyone who believes in Him won’t die, but will live forever.

16 “Because in the same way, God loves the people of the world enough to cause his only son to be born and to give him to the world, so that everyone who believes that will not die, but will live forever. 17 God didn’t send his son here to condemn poeple, but rather to save them. 18 The one who trusts in me will not be condemned. However, the one who doesn’t believe this has already been condemned because he rejected the authority of the only son that God has given birth to. 19 And this is the verdict: that light has come into the word, but some people loved the darkness more than the light because they do evil things. 20 Because everyone who does evil things hates the light, and doesn’t come to the light so that their actions won’t be exposed. 21 but whoever does what is right and true comes toward the light so that their actions can be revealed as being done with God’s help.”

5 [ref]Because of that, they came to a town in Samaria called Sukar, which was near the place Joseph had inherited from his father Jacob, 6 and Jacob’s well was there. Jesus was tired from all the walking and was sitting at the well around noon.

7 A woman came from the town to get water from the well, and Jesus asked her for a drink 8 since his followers had gone into the town to buy some lunch.

9 [ref]The woman said to him, “You’re a Jew. How come you’re asking me for a drink when I’m from Samaria?” (Jews don’t normally associate with anyone from Samaria.)

10 Jesus answered, “If you had recognised God’s gift and who it is who’s asking you for a drink, you would have asked me and I would have given you living water.”

11 But the woman countered, “Sir, you don’t even have rope and a bucket to reach the water in the deep well. How then could you get living water?” 12 I doubt that you’re greater than our ancestor Jacob who made this well for us, and drank this water along with his children and his animals.”

13 Jesus responded, “Everyone who drinks this water will get thirsty again, 14 but whoever drinks the water that I give them will find a well springing up inside them—springing into eternal life.”

15 The woman said, “Ok. Give me some of that water so that I won’t be thirsty and won’t have to keep coming out here to the well.”

16 Jesus said, “First go and get your husband and return here.”

17 But she retorted, “I don’t have a husband!”

And Jesus said, “True enough—indeed you don’t. 18 You’ve had five husbands and you’re not married to the guy man you’re living with, so that was true.”

19 The woman reacted, “Sir, I can tell that you’re a prophet. 20 Our ancestors worshipped on this mountain, but you Jews claim that people should only worship there in Jerusalem.”

21 Then Jesus told her, “You need to believe that I’m from God, woman, because a day is coming when everyone will be worshipping him neither on this mountain, nor in Jerusalem. 22 You people in Samaria don’t even know who you worship, but we do because people will be saved by means of the Jews. 23 But a time is coming, starting even now, when the true worshippers will be worshipping the father in spirit and truth, because those are the ones that he is wanting to worship him. 24 God is spirit, and the ones worshipping him must worship in spirit and totally honesty.”

25 The woman said, “I’m aware that the Messiah is coming—the chosen one of God. Whenever he appears, he’ll teach us everything.”

26 And Jesus responded, “I am the Messiah. Yes, he’s speaking to you!”

28 So the woman left her water jar there, and went back into the town. She told the people there, 29 “Come and see a man who knew as much about me as I know myself! He must be the Messiah!” 30 So they left the town and went out to see Jesus.

31 Meanwhile, Jesus’ followers encouraged him, “Teacher, here, eat.”

32 But he said, “I’ve had food to eat that you don’t know about.”

33 This made his followers ask each other, “Did you see anyone bring him something to eat?”

34 And Jesus explained, “Obeying God who sent me is like food to me, and I plan to finish his work.” 35 Don’t you all have a saying about needing to wait four months for a harvest? But I’m telling you that you all should open your eyes and look at the fields, because they’re already colouring with the coming harvest. 36 The harvester gets paid with a harvest that will last forever so that the ones who plant and the ones who harvest can all celebrate. 37 There’s truth in the statement that one person plants and a different person harvests. 38 I’ve sent you all out to harvest where you haven’t worked before—others did the groundwork and you benefit from their work.

39 And many people of Samaria came out of that town and believed that Jesus is God because the woman had told them that he knew everything she had done. 40 So when they all came, they asked him to stay in their town, and so they stayed there for two days.

41 And many more believed as a result of his teaching, 42 so they told the woman, “We no longer believe just because of what you said, but now that we’ve heard him for ourselves, we can see that he really is the saviour of the world.”

No, the one who’ll accuse you is Moses because he’s the one that you’re hoping you’ll please. 46 The trouble is that if you actually believed what Moses wrote, you would believe me, because Moses was writing about me. 47 But since you don’t really believe his writings, you’re not at all likely to believe me.”

6:1 The feeding of 5,000 families

(Mat. 14:13-21; Mar. 6:30-44; Luk. 9:10-17)

6After all this, Jesus boated across the lake. 2 A large crowd followed him because they had seen his miraculous healings of sick people. 3 Jesus climbed a hill and sat there with his apprentices. 4 At that time, it was getting close to the Jewish Feast of Passover. 5 When Jesus looked out and saw the large crowd coming, he asked Philip, “Where can we get enough food from to feed all this crowd?” 6 But he was only saying this to test Philip because he already knew what he was going to do.

7 Philip answered, “It would take quite a few months wages to buy even just a snack for this many people.”

8 Now one of Jesus’ apprentices, Simon Peter’s brother, Andrew, said to Jesus, 9 “There’s a boy here with five buns and two small cooked fish, but that wouldn’t even start to feed this crowd.”

10 But Jesus told them to get all the people to sit down as there was a large grassy area. So they all sat down—some five thousand men. 11 Then he took the buns and gave thanks to God, and started passing around both the buns and the fish, and everyone ate as much as they wanted! 12 Once everyone was full, Jesus got his apprentices to collect the left-over fragments so as not to waste anything. 13 So they gathered the left-overs and filled twelve baskets with left-over bread.

14 The people who had witnessed this and other miracles said, “This really is the prophet that God promised to send!” 15 But Jesus knew that they intended to come and grab him to announce him as their king, so he moved out and on up the hill.

6:16 Jesus comes walking across the lake

(Mat. 14:22-33; Mar. 6:45-52)

16 As it moved into evening, Jesus apprentices went back down to the lake 17 and having boarded the boat, they set out across the lake toward Capernaum. By then it was dark, and there was still no sign of Jesus. 18 Then a strong wind hit the lake and the water got rough. 19 When they had rowed about five kilometres, they saw Jesus approaching the boat walking on top of the lake, and they were very scared. 20 But he told them, “Don’t worry—it’s me.” 21 After that they were willing to let him into the boat, and immediately the boat was at their arrival place.

22 The next day, the crowd looked across the lake and saw that only one small boat had crossed, and they knew that only Jesus’ apprentices had boarded that boat and left, and not Jesus himself. 23 Other boats came across from Tiberias near the place where they had eaten the bread after the Master had given thanks. 24 So when all the people saw that Jesus wasn’t around and his apprentices had left, they went off looking for Jesus in Capernaum.

6:25 The bread that enables true life

25 When they found him there across the lake, they queried, “Teacher, when did you come here?”

26 And Jesus answered, “Honestly, I know that you’re looking for me, not because you saw miracles, but because you all had plenty of food to fill you up. 27 Don’t just work for the food which goes off, but for the food which lasts forever, which the Son of Man will give to you because God the Father has put his mark of approval on Him.”

28 Puzzled, they asked him, “What should we be doing if we want to do God’s work?”

29 He answered, “Doing God’s work is putting your trust in the one that He sent here.”

30 “If so,” they said, “what miracle will you do for us to see so that we can believe in you? What can you do? 31 [ref]Our ancestors ate the manna out in the wilderness, as Moses wrote, ‘He gave them bread from heaven to eat.’ ”

32 Jesus said to them, “Actually I can tell you that it wasn’t Moses who gave them bread from heaven, but my father gives you the real bread from heaven, 33 because the bread of God is the one who came down from heaven and who gives life to the world.”

34 So they requested, “Sir, always give us that bread.”

35 But Jesus answered, “I am the bread that gives life—whoever comes to me will never hunger, and the one believing in me, will never be thirsty again. 36 But as I said already, although you have seen me you refuse to believe me. 37 Everyone that the Father gives to me will come to me, and I certainly won’t turn away anyone who comes to me, 38 because I came down from heaven, not to do what I want, but to do what the one who sent me wants. 39 And what He wants me to not lose any that he’s given to me, but to bring them back to life on the last day. 40 My father wants everyone who watches me and believes in me to live forever, because I will bring them back to life on that last day.”

41 This little speech made the religious leaders grumble about him because he had said that he was the food that came down from heaven, 42 as they figured out, “This is Jesus the some of Joseph and so we know his parents. So how can he say that he come down from heaven?”

43 Jesus scolded them, “Don’t be grumbling to each other. 44 No one can join me unless the Father who sent me attracts them,and then I’ll be bringing them back to life on the last day.” 45 [ref]The prophets wrote that the people will be taught by God. And everyone who’s heard and learned from the Father comes to me. 46 Not that any person has seen the Father—only the one who came from God has seen Him. 47 I tell you sincerely: the one who believes in me will live forever. 48 I am the bread that gives true life. 49 Your ancestors ate manna in the wilderness, but they all went on to die. 50 I am the bread that came down from heaven and whoever eats this will not die. 51 I am the living bread that came down from heaven and if anyone eats this bread, he will live through to the next age.”

52 At this point, the religious leaders began to argue with each other, “How could this man give us his flesh to eat?”

53 So Jesus explained, “Indeed if you don’t eat my flesh and drink my blood, you aren’t truly alive! 54 The person who eats my flesh and drinks my blood will gain eternal life—I will bring them back to life on the last day. 55 For my flesh is the true food, and my blood is the true drink. 56 Whoever eats my flesh and drinks my blood lives in me, and I will live in him. 57 The father is alive and sent me here, and I am alive because of Him, so also the person who eats me will be alive because of me. 58 Unlike your ancestors who ate that bread and then died, whoever eats this bread that came down from heaven will live through to the next age.”

6:59 Many people find the teaching too difficult

59 Jesus taught all of this in the synagogue there in Capernaum. 60 But even many of Jesus followers who heard it said, “This is difficult teaching—it’s too hard to take.”

61 But Jesus already knew inside that his followers were grumbling about this and asked them, “Do you find this offensive? 62 What if you were to see the Son of Man ascending to where he came from? 63 It’s the spirit that is the source of life, not the body! The concepts that I’ve taught you are all spiritual and life-giving. 64 But there are some here who don’t believe me.” Actually, Jesus had already known from the beginning just who would believe, and which person would betray him. 65 So he said, “That’s why I told you that no one can come to me unless the Father has given them to me.”

66 After this, many of his followers went back and no longer accompanied him around.

67 So Jesus said to the twelve, “Don’t you also want to leave?”

68 [ref]Simon answered, “Master, if we went, who would we follow then? You’re the only one who teaches about living forever. 69 We believe and know that you are the holy son of God.”

70 Jesus responded, “Didn’t I choose all twelve of you myself? And yet one of you is a devil.” 71 (He was talking about Judas, son of Simon Iskariot, because he was the one who would go on to betray him.)
