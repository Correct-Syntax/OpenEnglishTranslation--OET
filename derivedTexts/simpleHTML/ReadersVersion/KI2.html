Note: This is still a very early look into the unfinished text of the Open English Translation of the Bible. Please double-check the text in advance before using in public.

Note: This Readers’ Version is a translation into contemporary English aimed at the person on the street who hasn’t necessarily been brought up with exposure to Biblical jargon and/or 16th century English. It’s designed to be used alongside the Literal Version which gives the English reader a window into what’s actually written in the original languages. (See the introduction for more details—we recommend that you read the introduction first if you’re wanting to read and understand the Literal Version.) Between the two versions you should be able to easily read the clear message of the text, while at the same time being able to check for yourself if that’s a fair translation of what the original authors had inked onto their ancient manuscripts.

2 Kings

Introduction

The Igkarangeb Hari sumpul te story concerning the daruwa migharian te me Israilita ware neimpusi diye te Hun‑a Hari. This Libru nabaad te daruwa: 1.) The story concerning the daruwa migharian puun te egliwaraanan ka igkasiyam siglu te ware pad neetew si Jesus until natalu e ka Samarya, and nataman this te neimpus e ka migharian diye te igkahibang balabahan te leg‑un 722 te ware pad neetew si Kristu. 2.) The story concerning the migharian te Huda puun te pegkatalu te migharian te Israel until pegkalundus and te pegkahuhus te Jerusalem pinaahi ki Hari Nibukadnisar Hari te Babilunya te leg‑un 586 te ware pad neetew si Kristu. Diye neimpusi ka story te neyimu e gubirnadur si Gidalya diye te Huda diralem te pegmandu te Babilunya, and te impaligwe e si Hari Hihuwakin naprisu diye te Babilunya.

This me karereetan, neyitabu diye te nasud te Israel puun su ware migtuman ka me Hari and ka me keet-etawan te Huda te God. The pegkahuhus te Jerusalem and ka pegkasakup te me keet-etawan te Huda iyan in-isip pinakarakel neyitabu te story te Israel.

Si Prupita Ilisyu ka amana neila here te Igkarangeb Hari. Si Ilisyu ka migsubal ki prupita Ilyas.

Main components of this “book”

The nabaad peghari (1:1–17:41)

a. The prupita si Ilisyu (1:1–8:15)

b. The me Hari te Huda and te Israel (8:16–17:4)

c. The pegkatalu te Samarya (17:5-41)

The peghari te Huda (18:1–25:30)

a. Ligkat ki Isikiyas peendiye te ki Husiyas (18:1–21:26)

b. The peghari of Husiyas (22:1–23:30)

c. The katamanan Hari te Huda (23:31–24:20)

d. The pegkatalu te Jerusalem (25:1-30)

It is our prayer that this Readers’ Version of the Open English Translation of the Bible will give you clear understanding of the accounts and messages written by the God-inspired Biblical writers.
