Note: This is still a very early look into the unfinished text of the Open English Translation of the Bible. Please double-check the text before using in public.

Note: This Readers’ Version is a translation into contemporary English aimed at the person on the street who hasn’t necessarily been brought up with exposure to Biblical jargon and/or King James English. It’s designed to be used alongside the Literal Version which gives the English reader a window into what’s actually written in the original languages. (See the introduction for more details—we recommend that you read the introduction first if you’re wanting to read and understand the Literal Version.) Between the two versions you should be able to easily read the clear message of the text, while at the same time being able to check for yourself if that’s a fair translation of what the original authors had inked onto their ancient manuscripts.

Ezra

Introduction

The baseen of Ezra, hendue te sumpul red te Me baseen te Krunikas. Nahuhud kayi ka concerning the peg-uli te duma me Hudiyu naddakep diye te Babilunya, and ka pegpalibed te keugpe and pegsimba diye te Jerusalem. Nabaad seini te tatelu: 1.) The pegpeuli of Siru Hari te Pirsya te seeye se hun‑a punduk te me Hudiyu naddakep diyad te Babilunya. 2.) The pegbalew-balew pegpasasindeg te temple and ka peghalad kayi, and ka pegpalibed te pegsimba te God diye te Jerusalem. 3.) The peg-uli diye te Jerusalem te seeye se lein e man‑e punduk te me Hudiyu impanguluwan of Ezra, ka sabeka mangngateu te Balaud te God. Nakabulig sikandin te me Israilita te pegpalibed te me belief(s) and nabatasan dan, eyew kene dan egkeengkeran ka pegsimba te malehet God.

Main components of this “book”

The hun‑a punduk te me Hudiyu nekeuli puun te pegkarakep 1:1-2:70

The pegbalew-balew pegpasasindeg te temple and ka peghalad kayi 3:1-6:22

The peg-uli of Ezra and te duma naddakep 7:1-10:44
