Note: This is still a very early look into the unfinished text of the Open English Translation of the Bible. Please double-check the text before using in public.

Note: This Readers’ Version is a translation into contemporary English aimed at the person on the street who hasn’t necessarily been brought up with exposure to Biblical jargon and/or King James English. It’s designed to be used alongside the Literal Version which gives the English reader a window into what’s actually written in the original languages. (See the introduction for more details—we recommend that you read the introduction first if you’re wanting to read and understand the Literal Version.) Between the two versions you should be able to easily read the clear message of the text, while at the same time being able to check for yourself if that’s a fair translation of what the original authors had inked onto their ancient manuscripts.

Matthew’s account

Introduction

The migsulat te seini baseen, iyan si Matiyu egngaranan ded ki Libi. Sabeka sikandin te sapulu and daruwa (12) follower of Jesus, and egngaranan ki Alpiyu ka amey rin. Matig-Galiliya sikandin piru diye migtrabahu te Kapirnaum isip talagsukut te buhis te ware pad sikandin neyimu follower of Jesus. Ne kema ke si Jesus buwad e ka migbalew-balew te ngaran din Libi te neyimu e sikandin follower, and migngaranan e sikandin ki Matiyu iyan igpasabut te Hebrews linalahan ‘ka dasag te God.’

The story of Matiyu, diye migbunsud te itulan te kaap-apuan of Jesus, te pegkeetew rin, pegbewutismu and te pegtintal kandin, te pegwali and te pegpanulu din, and te pegpamawi din te egmanderalu diye te Galiliya. Pegkapenga kayi, nahuhud e man‑e of Matiyu ka concerning the peggendiye of Jesus te Jerusalem, and ka nangimu rin te diyad sikandin. Nahuhud din man‑e ka neyitabu te katamanan simana of Jesus diye te Jerusalem, ka peglansang kandin te krus, and ka pegkeuyag din.

Nahuhud te seini baseen si Jesus, iyan ka Kristu insaad te God egluwas te me Hudiyu, and te langun kene me Hudiyu egpalintutuu and egsalig kandin. Ne pinaahi kandin, natuman te God ka insaad din diye te keet-etawan din sumale te Tapey Kasabutan.

Seini se Meupiya Panugtulen, migpeila si Jesus ka mabantug Talagpanulu, due katenged din te pegpasabut te Balaud te God. Kayi te seini baseen, due lalimma malayat pegpanulu of Jesus insulat of Matiyu, and naan pa iya ka pegpanulu of Jesus concerning the Peghari te God: (1) The pegwali diye te bubungan concerning the pegbebatasan te people, ka me katungdanan and ka egkengarawat dan, and ka egtamanan te people nasakup te Migharian te God (Kapitulu 5–7); (2) ka pegpaney-paney te sapulu and daruwa (12) apustulis concerning the me himu ran (Kapitulu 10); (3) ka me allegory concerning the Peghari te God (Kapitulu 13); (4) ka pegpanulu concerning the kaluwasan te egkeyimu follower (Kapitulu 18); and (5) ka pegpanulu concerning the katammanan te seini timpu and ka concerning the egginguma Peghari te God. (Kapitulu 24–25).

8:28 Jesus banishes demons into a herd of pigs

(Mar. 5:1-20; Luk. 8:26-39)

28 And when Jesus arrived at the country of the Gadarenes on the other side of the lake, he was met by two very dangerous demon-possessed men who were coming out of the cemetary. Because of them, some people weren’t able to travel on that road. 29 Suddenly the two men yelled out saying, “Son of God, what’s the connection between us and you? Have you come here to cause us pain before the designated time?”

30 Now there was a herd of many pigs grazing not far away from them, 31 and the demons begged Jesus saying, “If you do drive us out, send us into the herd of pigs.”

32 And he said to them, “Depart!” So they came out and went away into the pigs, and next thing all the herd rushed down the bank into the lake and drowned in the water.

33 Then the men who had been grazing them took off, and when they got to the city they reported everything, including what had happened to the demon-possessed men. 34 Soon all the city came out to meet with Jesus, and when they found him they asked him to leave their region.

9:1 Jesus forgives and heals a paralysed man

(Mar. 2:1-12; Luk. 5:17-26)

9So Jesus got into a boat and crossed back over to his own city of Capernaum. 2 Some people there brought a paralysed man to him lying on a stretcher. And when Jesus saw their faith, he said to the man, “Son, be encouraged. Your sins are forgiven.”

3 Immediately some of the teachers of the law said among themselves, “This man presumes to be God.”

4 But Jesus knew their thoughts and said, “Why are you thinking evil things in your hearts? 5 Do you think it’s easier to say, ‘Your sins are forgiven,’ or to say, ‘Stand up and walk’? 6 But so that you will know that the son of man has the authority here on earth to forgive sins,” he said, “stand up, take your stretcher, and go to your house.”

7 Then the man stood up and went off to his house. 8 The crowds were struck with fear after seeing this, yet they praised God for giving such authority to men.

9:9 Jesus calls Matthew and they dine with sinners

(Mark 2:13-17; Luke 5:27-32)

9 And as Jesus was leaving there, he saw a man called Matthew (also known as Levi) sitting in the tax office. He said to Matthew, “Follow me.” And he got up and followed him.

10 [ref]Later when Jesus was eating at Matthew’s house, many tax collectors and sinners came in and sat at the table together with Jesus and his disciples. 11 The Pharisees noticed this and said to his disciples, “How come your teacher eats with tax collectors and sinners?”

12 But Jesus heard this and said, “People who are well don’t need a doctor—only the ones with problems do. 13 [ref]So go and find out the meaning of this: I like mercy not sacrifice. Because it’s sinners that I came to call, not those considered righteous.”

9:14 Jesus is asked about fasting

(Mark 2:18-22; Luke 5:33-39)

14 Then the disciples of John approached Jesus and said, “How come that we and the Pharisees fast regularly, but your disciples don’t fast?”

15 And Jesus replied to them, “Surely the groomsmen aren’t sad while the groom is with them? But the time will come when the groom has to leave them and then they will fast.

16 No one patches old clothes with new material because the patch will shrink and the clothes would end up with a worse hole. 17 And they don’t put new wine into an old container, otherwise the container could split and be ruined and the wine would be lost. Rather they put new wine into a new container and both survive.”

(Mark 5:21-43; Luke 8:40-56)

19 So Jesus and his disciples got up and followed him.

23 Now when Jesus went into the house of the ruler, he saw the funeral musicians and the distressed people 24 and he said, “Go away. The girl hasn’t died—she’s sleeping.” But they ridiculed him. 25 However when the crowd was moved out, he went in and took her hand, and the girl got up. 26 Then the news of this spread all around that region.
