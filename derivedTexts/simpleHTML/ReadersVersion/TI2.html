Note: This is still a very early look into the unfinished text of the Open English Translation of the Bible. Please double-check the text in advance before using in public.

Note: This Readers’ Version is a translation into contemporary English aimed at the person on the street who hasn’t necessarily been brought up with exposure to Biblical jargon and/or 16th century English. It’s designed to be used alongside the Literal Version which gives the English reader a window into what’s actually written in the original languages. (See the introduction for more details—we recommend that you read the introduction first if you’re wanting to read and understand the Literal Version.) Between the two versions you should be able to easily read the clear message of the text, while at the same time being able to check for yourself if that’s a fair translation of what the original authors had inked onto their ancient manuscripts.

2 Timothy

Introduction

The igkarangeb sulat of Paul ki Timutiyu, diye din sulata te Ruma te timpu naprisu sikandin tenged te pegwali rin concerning ki Hisu Kristu. Inhangyu of Paul si Timutiyu egpeendiyeen te kandin te kene pad sikandin eggimatayan. Ne si Timutiyu, diye pad sikandin te Ipisu ka egpanulu te believers.

Kayi te this sulat, intambahan of Paul si Timutiyu eglig‑en eyew egpakaantus sikandin egwali and eggimu te impeyimu te God kandin minsan taliware te me peg-antus and me pegsupak te duma person. Impanpanayan din again si Timutiyu concerning the mareet egkeyingumaan te “keungelan and te ware karuan apulanan” su egpakareet this te people egpammineg.

Labew te langun, impasuman-suman of Paul si Timutiyu concerning the kandin umul su eyew egkeyimu allegory of Timutiyu ka pegpalintutuu of Paul, ka pegpariralem din, ka peggeyinawa rin, ka pegpabulus din, and ka peg-antus din taliware te me pegbayad-bayad kandin.

Main components of this “book”

Introduction 1:1-2

The pegpasalamat and ka pegbanasal 1:3-2:13

The tambag and ka pegpaney-paney 2:14-4:5

The peg-ugpe of Paul 4:6-18

The katammanan 4:19-22

It is our prayer that this Readers’ Version of the Open English Translation of the Bible will give you clear understanding of the accounts and messages written by the God-inspired Biblical writers.
