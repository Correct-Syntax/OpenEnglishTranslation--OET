Note: This is still a very early look into the unfinished text of the Open English Translation of the Bible. Please double-check the text before using in public.

Note: This Readers’ Version is a translation into contemporary English aimed at the person on the street who hasn’t necessarily been brought up with exposure to Biblical jargon and/or King James English. It’s designed to be used alongside the Literal Version which gives the English reader a window into what’s actually written in the original languages. (See the introduction for more details—we recommend that you read the introduction first if you’re wanting to read and understand the Literal Version.) Between the two versions you should be able to easily read the clear message of the text, while at the same time being able to check for yourself if that’s a fair translation of what the original authors had inked onto their ancient manuscripts.

1 Chronicles

Introduction

The An-anayan and ka Ikarangeb Baseen te Krunikas migguhud ded degma te Baseen of Samwil and te Baseen te Me Hari piru nahuhud te lein paahi. Duen de daruwa impurtanti amana tuyu ke mania te nasulat ka nabaybayaan te me matig-Israel and ka pegmandu te me Hari:

1.) Eyew egkanengnengan te talagbasa minsan pad te kareetan nabayaan te Huda and te Israel, intuman ded te God ka saad din and ka implanu rin diye te keet-etawan. Impamalehetan seini te talagsulat pinaahi te pegpangguhud din te neneyimu of Dabid, of Solomon, of Hihusapat, of Isikiyas, of Husiyas, and diye te people matinumanen te God.

2.) Eyew te pegpahunsiling diye te keet-etawan ke egmenuwen te egsimba ka God diye te temple rin diye te Jerusalem labew te langun diye te me talagpanubad and me Libita iyan eggimu te pegsimba. Ne migpataha man‑e seini si Dabid ka malehet pabunsuranan te temple minsan ke si Solomon e ka nakapasasindeg kayi.
