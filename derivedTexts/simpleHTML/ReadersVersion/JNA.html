Note: This is still a very early look into the unfinished text of the Open English Translation of the Bible. Please double-check the text before using in public.

Note: This Readers’ Version is a translation into contemporary English aimed at the person on the street who hasn’t necessarily been brought up with exposure to Biblical jargon and/or King James English. It’s designed to be used alongside the Literal Version which gives the English reader a window into what’s actually written in the original languages. (See the introduction for more details—we recommend that you read the introduction first if you’re wanting to read and understand the Literal Version.) Between the two versions you should be able to easily read the clear message of the text, while at the same time being able to check for yourself if that’s a fair translation of what the original authors had inked onto their ancient manuscripts.

Jonah

Introduction

The seini baseen, migpangguhud concerning the sabeka prupita egngaranan ki Hunas. Diye sikandin mig-ugpe te Israel te igkahibang balabahan, me 250 leg‑un ka miglihad te nekeimpus e si Dabid ka mighari. The seini baseen, kene iling te duma me story concerning the pegpanagne, su migpanengneng seini concerning the neula-ula te sabeka prupita ware migtuman te insuhu te God kandin egpeendiyeen te siyudad te Ninibi. Ware sikandin migtuman te insuhu kandin su nakataha sikandin kene egbulusan te God te eglegpad ka seeye siyudad ke eg-inniyug ka people te me sale dan. Piru tenged te kein-inuwan natamanan din, napehes sikandin miggendiye te Ninibi and migpangguhud te igpahuhud kandin te God. Nataman, neenenduenan sikandin and nasakitan puun su ware iya natuman ka ingkahi rin diye te people eglegparan te God ka inged dan.

Nahuhud degma te seini baseen egpalintutuu te God ka langun inggimu rin iling te weyig, ngalap, wind and duma pad. Piru labew te langun, nahuhud kayi hiid‑uwen ka God and dakel ka geyinawa rin, su minsan ka kuntere te keet-etawan din, impasaylu rin and inluwas din te mig-inniyug e sikandan te me sale dan.

1:1 Jonah=P tries to run from God

1[ref]Now Yahweh=GYHWH said to Jonah=P (Heb: Yonah) the son of Amittai=P, 2 “Go now to the major city, Nineveh=L, and speak out against it because I’ve noticed how evil they are.”

3 But Jonah=P decided to run away from Yahweh=GYHWH so he went down to Joppa=L (Heb: Yafo) and found a ship about to go to Tarshish=L. He paid the fare and went aboard to go with them to Tarshish=L to get away from Yahweh=GYHWH.

4 However Yahweh=GYHWH caused a strong wind on the sea and it became a such big storm that they all thought the ship would disintegrate. 5 The sailors were afraid and they each cried to their own gods for help, and they threw the cargo overboard to make the ship lighter. But Jonah=P had gone down into the hold where he had laid down and fallen fast asleep.

6 So the ship’s captain approached him and said, “Why are you sleeping? Come on, pray to your God and perhaps he will take notice of us so we won’t die.”

7 Then the sailors said to each other, “Let’s draw straws to find out whose fault it is that we are in this predicament.” So they drew straws and Jonah=P was indicated. 8 So they said to him, “Hey, tell us why this disaster is happening to us. What is your occupation? Where are you from? What country? What nationality are you?”

9 And he said to them, “I’m a Hebrew and I fear Yahweh=GYHWH the God of the heavens who made the sea and the land.”

10 And the men became really scared and asked him what he had done, because he had told them that he was running from Yahweh.

11 So they asked him what they should do to him so that the sea would calm, because the storm was getting worse.

12 And he said to them, “Pick me up and throw me into the sea so that the sea will calm down because I know that this terrible storm came because of me.”

13 However, the men tried to row but they were not able to return to the land because the sea was very rough and getting worse. 14 So they called to Yahweh=GYHWH and said, “We beg you Yahweh: please don’t let us die because of this man, and don’t consider us guilty because you yourself have made this happen.” 15 And then they picked up Jonah=P and threw him into the sea, and the sea became calm. 16 The men felt really scared of Yahweh=GYHWH and they sacrificed to him and made vows.

17 [ref]Then Yahweh=GYHWH commanded a huge fish to swallow Jonah=P and he stayed in the belly of the fish for three days and three nights.

2:1 Jonah’s prayer

2And Jonah=P prayed to Yahweh=GYHWH his God from the belly of the fish. 2 And he said,

“I called to you Yahweh=GYHWH in my distress and you answered me. I cried for help from the valley of death and you heard my voice. 3 You have thrown me right into the deep—the heart of the sea; and a flood surrounds me. All your waves are crashing over me.

4 And I said, ‘I have been driven out of your sight however I will look again towards your holy temple.’

5 I was up to my neck in the waters; it was deep all around me. There were reeds wrapped around my head.

6 I went down to the foundations of the mountains; barred from returning to the land. But you saved my life from the grave, Yahweh=GYHWH my God.

7 When my life was draining away from me, I remembered you, Yahweh, and my prayer reached you in your holy temple.

8 Those who worship lifeless gods abandon their allegiance to you.

9 But I’ll speak out my gratitude as I sacrifice to you. I’ve vowed this and I’ll do it, because Yahweh=GYHWH is my rescuer.”

10 Then Yahweh=GYHWH spoke to the fish and it vomitted Jonah=P up onto the land.

3:1 Jonah=P goes to Nineveh

3And Yahweh=GYHWH spoke to Jonah=P a second time saying, 2 “Go now to the major city, Nineveh, and announce this declaration which I’ve told you.”

3 So Jonah=P went directly to Nineveh. It was a large city, taking three days to travel across. 4 [ref]Jonah=P went one day’s travel into the city and publicly announced, “Nineveh is going to be destroyed in forty days!”

5 The people of Nineveh believed in God and announced a time of fasting, and from the least to the most important people, they just wore sackcloth as a sign of contrition.

6 The king of Nineveh also heard about it and he got down from his throne, took off his robe and put on sackcloth, then sat in a pile of ashes. 7 And he had a statement written out and publicly read throughout Nineveh, saying, “The king and his advisors declare that people and livestock, be it herds or flocks, are prohibited from grazing or eating anything, and even from drinking water. 8 Both people and livestock should just wear sacks, and they should use their strength to cry out to God and they should turn away from the evil and violent things they have been doing. 9 And maybe God will turn and change his mind and stop his fierce anger, and maybe we won’t die.”

10 And God saw their actions in turning away from their evil ways and he changed his mind about the destruction which he had said he would send to them and he didn’t proceed with it.

4:1 Jonah=P gets upset

4But Jonah=P was very upset and the situation made him angry. 2 [ref]And he said to Yahweh, “Please Yahweh, wasn’t this what I said would happen when I was still in my own country? That’s why I fled to Tarshish the first time, because I knew that you are a caring forgiving god, who doesn’t get angry quickly; full of kindess and not wanting to cause distress. 3 [ref]So therefore Yahweh, please kill me because I’d rather die than live like this.”

4 But Yahweh=GYHWH said, “Do you think it’s right to be angry like that?”

5 And Jonah=P went outside the city to the east and made himself a shelter and sat there in the shade to see what would happen to the city. 6 And Yahweh=GYHWH God commanded a plant and it grew up over Jonah=P to shade his head and to relieve him from his distress. Jonah=P was very pleased about the plant. 7 Then at dawn the next day God commanded a grub and it attacked the plant which then shrivelled up. 8 And when the sun came up, God sent a hot east wind and the sun beat on Jonah’s head. He became faint and asked for his life to end and said, “It’s better for me to die than to live.”

9 And God said to Jonah, “Do you think it’s right to be angry about the plant?”

And Jonah=P replied, “Yes, I’m angry enough to die!”

10 Then Yahweh=GYHWH said, “You felt sorry for the plant which you didn’t even plant or work for. It came up in a day and died again in a day 11 So shouldn’t I feel sorry for the huge city of Nineveh which has so many people in it who don’t know right from wrong, over 120,000 people, not to mention all the livestock!”
