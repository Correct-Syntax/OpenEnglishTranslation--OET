Note: This is still a very early look into the unfinished text of the Open English Translation of the Bible. Please double-check the text before using in public.

Note: This Readers’ Version is a translation into contemporary English aimed at the person on the street who hasn’t necessarily been brought up with exposure to Biblical jargon and/or King James English. It’s designed to be used alongside the Literal Version which gives the English reader a window into what’s actually written in the original languages. (See the introduction for more details—we recommend that you read the introduction first if you’re wanting to read and understand the Literal Version.) Between the two versions you should be able to easily read the clear message of the text, while at the same time being able to check for yourself if that’s a fair translation of what the original authors had inked onto their ancient manuscripts.

Isaiah

Introduction

Si Isayas anak of Amus ka migsulat te seini baseen. Sabeka sikandin prupita te God, and diye sikandin mig-ugpe te Jerusalem. The kaluwasan te ngaran ‘Isayas’, ‘The Magbebaye God ka Egluwas.’ Nasulat ka seini baseen te me leg‑un 740 peendiye te 680 te ware pad neetew si Jesus. Ne nabaad seini te tatelu baad:

1.) Kapitulu 1–39: Nahuhud kayi ka concerning the timpu ingginalatan te Asirya ka Huda, ka migharian diye te igkakawanan balabahan. Ne nakita of Isayas tenged bes te sale te keet-etawan, te pegkamasinupaken dan, and te kulang ka pegsalig dan te God, ka egpuunan ke mania te migginalatan te Asirya ka Huda. Impaay-ayaran panulua of Isayas ka keet-etawan and ka me pangulu ran eg-ugpe sikandan matareng se hinimuwan and eleg se peggukum. Impanpanayan din sikandan emun ke kene sikandan egpammineg te lalag te God, egkengareetan iya sikandan. Piru intagne man‑e of Isayas ka pegkeetew te descendants of Dabid te egginguma timpu iyan likes eg-ikulen te keet-etawan, and egginguma ka timpu due kalinaw te intiru world.

2.) Kapitulu 40–55: Natagne kayi ka concerning the egginguma pegkarakep and pegkeuyan te masulug keet-etawan te Huda peendiye te Babilunya. Ne impanengneng of prupita Isayas minsan iling dutu, kene egmahuye ka keet-etawan kene egkaawaan te egkapallateng su egluwasen ded te God ka keet-etawan din and igpalibed din ded sikandan diye te Jerusalem eyew egbunsud e man e te iyam peg-ugpe. The uluwan te seini me kapitulu, ka God ka egbuut te egkeyitabu and ka egplanu para te keet-etawan din, and miglagkes degma seini te katuyuan dan diye te langun nations egpakarawat te panalangin puun te God pinaahi te Israel.

3.) Kapitulu 56–66: Natagne degma kayi ka concerning the keugpe te people igpalibed ded te God diye te Jerusalem. Nahuhud of Isayas kayi ka me suhu te God concerning the matareng peg-ugpe, eleg peggukum, pegtuman te Aldew te Peggimeley, pegpanubad, and praying. Ne egkabasa te seini me kapitulu ka lalag inggamit of Jesus te pegpanengneng in-alam sikandin eyew te pegwali te meupiya story (Isayas 61:1-2; Lukas 4:18-19.)
