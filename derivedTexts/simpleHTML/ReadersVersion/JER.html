Note: This is still a very early look into the unfinished text of the Open English Translation of the Bible. Please double-check the text in advance before using in public.

Note: This Readers’ Version is a translation into contemporary English aimed at the person on the street who hasn’t necessarily been brought up with exposure to Biblical jargon and/or 16th century English. It’s designed to be used alongside the Literal Version which gives the English reader a window into what’s actually written in the original languages. (See the introduction for more details—we recommend that you read the introduction first if you’re wanting to read and understand the Literal Version.) Between the two versions you should be able to easily read the clear message of the text, while at the same time being able to check for yourself if that’s a fair translation of what the original authors had inked onto their ancient manuscripts.

Jeremiah

Introduction

Si prupita Hirimiyas, started te himu rin isip prupita te 627 leg‑un te ware pad neetew si Kristu and migpabulus until me leg‑un 580 te ware pad neetew si Kristu. Te malayat timpu te pegpamakey of Hirimiyas, impanpanayan din ka keet-etawan concerning the karereetan egginguma te nasud tenged te sins dan, and te pegsimba ran te me false gods. Ne nakita of Hirimiyas ka katumanan te intagne din te timpu nasakup e ka Jerusalem of Nibukadnisar Hari te Babilunya. Nakita rin again ka pegkahuhus te siyudad and temple, and ka pegkarakep te masulug keet-etawan duma te Hari dan diye te Babilunya. Intagne din again ka return te keet-etawan puun te pegkarakep dan, and ka pegbalew-balew te pegpasasindeg te nasud diye te tapey natahuan here.

The Baseen of Hirimiyas nabaad te lalimma: 1.) The calling ki Hirimiyas; 2.) ka inlalag te God for te nasud te Huda and te me pangulu here te timpu te peghari enni Husiyas, Huwakim, Hihuyakin and of Sidikiyas; 3.) ka langun nabaybayaan te people sumale te nasulat of Baruk sikritari of Hirimiyas, lagkes ka lein-lein me pegpanagne, and ka impurtanti neyitabu te umul of Hirimiyas; 4.) ka inlalag te Master God concerning the lein-lein lapu diye nations; and 5.) ka duma pad story concerning the pegkahuhus te Jerusalem, and te pegkaddakep te keet-etawan peendiye te Babilunya.

Hiid‑uwen person si Hirimiyas; amana rin geyinawei ka keet-etawan din, and igkeepes din ka eggukum kandan. Due me baad te baseen amana ka geyinawa rin te pegpangguhud concerning the me peg-antus din tenged su in-umew sikandin te God eyew egkeyimu prupita. Iling te hapuy diye te pusung din ka lalag te Master God, and kene din iya this igkeeles.

Nasulat again te this baseen egginguma ka timpu duen e iyam kasabutan. Ne ka sika kasabutan eg-ay-ayaran te keet-etawan te God and egtumanen dan, minsan pad ware talagpanulu egpasuman-suman kandan su igsulat man e this diye te me pusung dan. (31:31-34)

Main components of this “book”

The calling ki Hirimiyas 1:1-19

The me pegpanagne concerning the peghari of Husiyas, Huwakim, Huwakin, and of Sidikiyas 2:1-25:38

The nabaybayaan of Hirimiyas 26:1-45:5

The me pegpanagne for te nations 46:1-51:64

The pegkahuhus te Jerusalem 52:1-34

It is our prayer that this Readers’ Version of the Open English Translation of the Bible will give you clear understanding of the accounts and messages written by the God-inspired Biblical writers.
