Note: This is still a very early look into the unfinished text of the Open English Translation of the Bible. Please double-check the text in advance before using in public.

Note: This Readers' Version is a translation into contemporary English aimed at the person on the street who hasn't necessarily been brought up with exposure to Biblical jargon and/or 500-year old English. (It's designed to be used alongside the Literal Version which gives the English reader a window into what's actually written in the original languages. (See the introduction for more details—we recommend that you read the introduction first if you're wanting to read and understand the Literal Version.) Between the two versions you should be able to easily read the clear message of the text, while at the same time being able to check for yourself if that's a fair translation of what the original authors had inked onto their ancient manuscripts.)

Note that greyed words are words that the translators consider were most probably in the mind of the writer, but as none of us can double-check with the original speakers or writers, the reader is free to disagree. They are clearly marked because we have tried to be as honest / transparent as possible.

Deuteronomy

Introduction

The document of Deuteronomy, impaay-ayaran megsinsinundula stories of Moses there to descendants of Israel, there to inged of Muwab, the inged insanggelan dan puun of naluhey travelling ran there to disirtu, and of timpu mahaan e sikandan egseled and eg-ugpe there to Kanaan.

Ne this the duma important nahuhud of this document: 1.) Nahuhud here the pegpasuman-suman of Moses of descendants of Israel concerning the neneyitabu of nanlihad hep‑at pulu (40) leg‑un, and the peghangyu din of descendants of Israel of pegsuman-suman ke immenu sikandan of eg-inahak of Master there to disirtu. Ne nahuhud again here the peghangyu of Moses kandan eg-ikul and egmatinumanen of God. 2.) Nahuhud again here the pegguhud of Moses of Sapulu (10) Suhu and the explaining din of First Suhu, and the pegpanengneng din of descendants of Israel it’s necessary iyan dan just iya egpamakayen the Master God. Nalagkes nahuhud here the various law egpeikulan of descendants of Israel there to Kanaan. 3.) Nahuhud again here the pegpasuman-suman of Moses of descendants of Israel of igpasabut of kasabutan of God there to kandan, and the pegbagget din kandan of pegtuman of sika kasabutan. 4.) Nahuhud again here the peg-alam ki Josh. isip igliwan of teacher of people of God and the singing of Moses of peggale-gale of pegkamatinumanen of God of me saad din, and the blessing of descendants of Israel. Nahuhud again here the death of Moses there to Muwab, talipag of Hurdan dapit of igsile.

The mabantug theme of this document iyan the pegluwas and blessing of God of people chosen din and inggeyinawaan. Purisu correct iya egsumsumanen of descendants of Israel eggeyinawaan and egtahuren the God so that egpakaangken sikandan of umul, and not egkaawaan of blessing.

The very important verses here iyan the 6:4-6 and sika verses the said by Jesus labew of langun suhu miggenendue: Geyinawei niyu the God niyu of intiru pusung, gimukud, and keseg niyu.

Main components of this “book”

The first pegpakiglalag of Moses 1:1-4:49

The second pegpakiglalag of Moses 5:1-26:19

a. The Sapulu (10) Suhu 5:1-10:22

b. Laws, me tulumanen, and warnings 11:1-26:19

The pegbalew-balew of kasabutan 29:1-30:20

The final pegpakiglalag of Moses 31:1-33:29

The death of Moses 34:1-12
