Note: This is still a very early look into the unfinished text of the Open English Translation of the Bible. Please double-check the text in advance before using in public.

Note: This Readers' Version is a translation into contemporary English aimed at the person on the street who hasn't necessarily been brought up with exposure to Biblical jargon and/or 500-year old English. (It's designed to be used alongside the Literal Version which gives the English reader a window into what's actually written in the original languages. (See the introduction for more details—we recommend that you read the introduction first if you're wanting to read and understand the Literal Version.) Between the two versions you should be able to easily read the clear message of the text, while at the same time being able to check for yourself if that's a fair translation of what the original authors had inked onto their ancient manuscripts.)

Note that greyed words are words that the translators consider were most probably in the mind of the writer, but as none of us can double-check with the original speakers or writers, the reader is free to disagree. They are clearly marked because we have tried to be as honest / transparent as possible.

2 Thessalonians

Introduction

This letter, was written by Paul igpeendiye of group of believers there to Tisalunika (Me Himu 17:1-10). Napehes si Paul eg-awe there to Tisalunika tenged su due Jews neenenduenan of preaching rin there to non-Jews. Ne miggalin sikandin there to Kurintu and there din sulata the first and the second letter din of matig-Tisalunika.

Te that timpu, nalibeg the believers there to Tisalunika su due teaching nakasamuk kandan, su miglibed kun e the Master si Jesus the messiah. Tenged su nalibeg e sikandan, imbalabahan of Paul the sika teaching pinaahi of explaining din kandan of duen pad kasamuk and karereetan egginguma of not pad eglibed the Messiah. The eg-uyan due, that egkuntere ki the messiah kein-inuwan teacher named of “Etew Talag-apul of laws.”

Impanulu of apustul Paul the egbasa of this letter, it’s necessary egtanud-tanud of langun impanulu din kandan minsan pad of me difficulty and me peg-antus. Keilangan continue sikandan the eggimu for of keuyahan dan and eggimu of good, iling also of inggimu of Paul.

Main components of Paul’s letter

Introduction 1:1-2

The thanking of Paul of matig-Tisalunika and the praying dan 1:3-12

The teaching concerning the return of the messiah 2:1-17

The pegbanasal concerning the praying and of not egpapepehul 3:1-15

The ending 3:16-18
